Canadian Economy & Bank of Canada
Canada's economy is uniquely exposed to US trade policy, commodity cycles, and a heavily indebted consumer. The Bank of Canada often diverges from the Fed — when it does, CAD/USD moves and cross-border capital flows become the trade. Canadian banks (RBC, TD, BMO) are systemically important and proxy for housing health. For anyone in Toronto finance, understanding BoC policy vs Fed policy divergence is a core skill — it shows up in every rate desk, currency desk, and fixed income role at Bay Street firms.
